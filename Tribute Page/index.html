Subhas Chandra Bose


Subhas Chandra Bose, the fearless freedom fighter, played a pivotal role in India’s struggle for independence. His unwavering commitment, militant approach, and formation of the Indian National Army (INA) continue to inspire generations.

About the Legend

  • Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha. He was the 9th child of his parents.
  • Bose excelled academically and stood 2nd in the 1913 matriculation examination. He passed the Indian Civil Services (ICS) examination but resigned in 1921 to join the freedom movement.
  • Bose was a strong advocate for India’s complete independence. He formed the Congress Volunteer Corps and became the General Secretary of the Indian National Congress.
  • In 1942, Bose formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) in collaboration with Japan. His famous slogan was: “Tum Mujhe Khoon Do, Main Tumhe Azadi Dunga” (Give me blood, and I will give you freedom).
  • Bose was greatly influenced by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda and Ramkrishna Paramhansa.
  • The British put Bose under house arrest, but he managed to escape to Germany and then to Japan.
  • In Japan, Bose began the formation of the Indian National Army (INA) to fight alongside the Axis powers against the British.
  • Subhas Chandra Bose remains a symbol of courage, patriotism, and resilience in India’s struggle for independence. His contributions continue to inspire generations.
  • Bose’s approach differed from Mahatma Gandhi’s non-violence. He believed in direct action against the British.
  • Bose’s death remains a mystery. The plane crash in which he was traveling in Taiwan in 1945 is still debated.
